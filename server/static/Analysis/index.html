Analysis of Salaries by Major


Starting Median Salary by Major

Analysis: On looking at the graph which is sorted by starting salaries in descending order we can observe that Physician's Assistants have had very high starting salaries, followed by engineering majors. Nursing major have salaries higher than one might expect. Next in the line are majors such as Business Management, Political Science, Marketing. At the bottom, we have the art majors: Music, Drama, Art, followed by Education, Religion, and Spanish.


Mid Career Salaries by Major

Analysis : Let's add the (median) mid-career salary, which will be more important over the long-haul. When we plot the mid-career salaries, we see that majors carry generally the same pattern, but there are some shifts as well. Physicians Assistants, Nursing, and Nutrition majors all seem to have some decreases in the rankings.

Starting Vs Mid Career Salary by Major

Let's sort by the (median) mid-career salary, since it is more important. We will also have the median starting salary as a benchmark of potential interest.
git Analysis: We observe again that the Physicians Assistants, Nursing, and Nutrition majors have fallen in the rankings. Top money makers (by mid-career salaries) are the engineering majors, with Chemical Engineering at the top.

Comparing all mid career percentiles by major

Let's start by sorting and plotting the mid career salaries ,lets do that by plotting 10th,25th,75th,95th percentile mid career salaries.

Analysis: Again, we observe that the Engineering Majors earn the most money In general, looking at the medians,we observe that most people make between 50K and 100K as their annual salaries mid-way through their careers. For the top performers (75th percentile), that range would be between 75K and 125K. The best top performers (90th percentile.) get a take home salary between 100K and 200K yearly. It is interesting to observe that some majors have pretty conservative ranges of salary like Physician's Assistants and Nursing majors. When it comes to the arts majors of Music and Drama, the top performers do very well -- the top 10% performers (musicians, actors) do very well, making more money than the top 25% of performers in the engineering/math/science majors. The people who make the most money, the top 10% of performers, are Economics and Finance majors.
